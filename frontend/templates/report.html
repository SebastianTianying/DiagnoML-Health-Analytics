Reference Treatment for Pneumonia

If pneumonia is caused by bacteria, the main treatment is antibiotics.

  • In milder cases, antibiotics can be taken by mouth.
  • Some cases of bacterial pneumonia will require hospitalization for treatment.
  • Young children and the elderly are more likely to need to go to the hospital to receive intravenous antibiotics, medical care and respiratory therapy
Reference Treatment for Pneumonia

If a virus is causing your pneumonia, antibiotics will not help.

  • Antiviral medication may be given accordingly.
  • If the patient have an influenza virus, medications such as oseltamivir (Tamiflu), zanamivir (Relenza), orperamivir (Rapivab) may be prescribed. These drugs keep flu viruses from spreading in the body.
  • If RSV is the cause of pneumonia, a medication such as ribavirin (Virazole) may be prescribed.

However, most viral pneumonias do not have specific treatment.

Reference Treatment for SNV

CNV (Choroidal neovascularization) can be treated with several methods:

  • The primary treatment for CNV is injection of medications into the eye vitreous cavity. These medications are called anti-VEGF (Vascular Endothelial Growth Factor), because they block the activity of a substance in the body called VEGF, shown to be the common factor contributing to CNV.
  • CNV may also be treated with photodynamic therapy (PDT) coupled with a photosensitive drug such as verteporfin(Visudyne). The drug is given intravenously. It is then activated in the eye by a laser light. The drug destroys the new blood vessels, and prevents any new vessels forming by forming thrombi.
  • Laser photocoagulation has also been used for the treatment of CNV related to myopicmacular degeneration.

Reference Treatment for DME

Diabetic Macular Edema (DME) is an eye problem related to diabetes. There are several treatments available for DME. Some of them include:

  • Anti-VEGF injections into the eye which stop the activity of vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGF) to promote the growth of blood vessels
  • Steroid implants
  • Laser treatments such as focal laser treatment for focal DME and grid laser treatment for diffuse DME
  • Medications such as Avastin (bevacizumab), Lucentis (ranibizumab), and Ozurdex (dexamethasone) that are injected into your eyes by an ophthalmologist

It is important to note that doctors may also tackle what is causing DME, like high blood sugar or high blood pressure

Reference Treatment for DRUSEN

Drusen are yellow deposits under the retina. There is currently no treatment for drusen. However, certain steps may be taken to prevent additional drusen from forming and to slow the progression of Age-related Macular Degeneration (AMD), which can be associated with soft drusen. Some of these steps include:

  • Taking certain vitamins and minerals that may help reduce drusen accumulation
  • Following a healthy diet with antioxidant vitamins found in fruits and vegetables that contribute to eye health
  • Not smoking or quitting smoking if you do smoke
  • Managing other medical conditions that may affect eye health

    • In rare cases where choroidal neovascular membranes (new blood vessels growing under the retina) develop, treatment with Anti-VEGF drugs may be appropriate to prevent the development of abnormal or leaking blood vessels in the retina
